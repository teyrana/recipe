Mulligatawny Soup
====

Indian-spiced mulligatawny soup smells heavenly while cooking in your kitchen. Apple adds a touch of sweetness using lite coconut milk keeps the calories low.

- Prep Time:    20 minutes
- Cook Time:    30 minutes
- Servings:      6 people

## Ingredients
- 3 cup vegetable broth or water for sauteeing
- 1 C uncooked red lentils rinsed and cleaned
- 1 large yellow onion chopped
- 1 large carrot peeled and diced
- 2 small or 1 large  potato peeled and diced
- 3 cl garlic, minced
- 2 t fresh ginger, peeled and minced
- 1 large apple peeled, cored and diced
- 15 oz diced tomatoes (or 1/4 C tomato paste)
- 1 T curry powder
- 0.5 t ground coriander
- 0.5 t ground cinnamon
- 0.5 t ground turmeric
- 0.25 t ground cardamom
- 0.25 t freshly ground black pepper
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 2/3 cup canned unsweetened lite coconut milk (or use 2/3 non-dairy milk mixed with about - 1 t coconut extract)
- salt and black pepper to taste

### Optional:
- 1 lb skinless, boneless chicken breast
- 1T vegetable oil (for chicken)
- 1/2 C chopped celery
- 1/2 C chopped green bell pepper
- 1 t ground ginger
- 1/2 C mango chutney
- 1/2 t crushed red pepper (or cayenne pepper)

### Garnish:
- 1/4 C chopped cilantro and/or scallions for garnish
- 0.5(?)  C chopped roasted salted cashews


## Instructions
[ 1. heat 1 t oil in a dutch oven over medium high heat. add chicken and saute 3 minutes. remove from pan, set aside.)
1. Add the onion and carrot to a large pot with ¼ cup water or vegetable broth over medium heat, then sauté for 4 to 5 minutes, or until the onions have softened.
2. Add the potatoes, garlic, ginger, apples, and diced tomatoes to the pot. Sauté for another 3 minutes, then add all of the spices and toss to coat.
3. Add the lentils and broth and let the contents come to a boil. Turn the heat down to medium-low and simmer uncovered for 30 minutes. 4. Add more water or broth if it gets too thick.
5. Transfer about half of the soup, slightly cooled, into a blender and blend until smooth. Then pour back into the pot and stir well. Or use an immersion blender to cream part of the soup in the pot.
6. Stir in the coconut milk and cook a few more minutes.
7. Taste, and adjust salt and black pepper as needed.
8. Serve topped with scallions along with crusty bread or naan for dipping.
