# Mushroom Risotto

- Prep Time:  20 mins
- Cook Time:  40 mins
- Total Time:  1 hr


## Ingredients
 
 
### Risotto

- 1 cup Arborio rice
- 2 T butter, divided in two
- 1 T extra virgin olive oil
- 1/2 C dry white wine
- 4 C beef stock, warmed up (I use Knorr bouillon cubes)
- 1/2 C freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano
- 1/2 t freshly cracked black pepper
- ?? Kosher salt, as needed

### Mushrooms

- 8 oz baby portobello (aka baby bellas, cremini, etc), thinly sliced to ¼"
- 1 T butter
- 2 T extra virgin olive oil
- 1/4 cup shallots or 1 small yellow onion, chopped
- 1 T minced garlic
- 1 T soy sauce
- 1 t fresh or dried thyme and/or sage
- 1/2 t kosher salt
- 1/2 t black pepper
- 1/2 cup roughly chopped flat leaf Italian parsley

## Instructions

### Basic Risotto

1. Heat 1 tablespoon butter and 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil in a saucepan until you hear a light sizzle and the butter begins to bubble.
  (1 T butter)
  (1 T olive oil)

2. Add 1 cup of Arborio rice and stir evenly and thoroughly.
    1 cup Arborio rice
3. After a minute or so - when the rice gets glossy (not brown), add ½ cup of dry white wine and keep stirring. 
    ½ cup dry white wine
4. Once the wine starts to bubble, it will be absorbed quickly (within about 2 minutes), so make sure you have 4 cups of beef stock warmed and ready. Add one ladle of stock at a time and stir. Adjust the heat so it's barely boiling. Just a couple bubbles here and there.
    4 cups beef stock, warmed up (I use Knorr bouillon cubes)
5. Once the liquid is absorbed, add another ladle of stock, stir, and repeat. Watch, smell, and taste. From adding the first ladle to the last, it takes about 25 minutes from start to finish. Taste, but be careful with adding salt too soon. The stock will add additional salt, and so will the salty Parmigiano Reggiano. 
6. When the rice is done, add (at least) 1 tablespoon butter and (at least) ½ cup of Parmigiano Reggiano (the good stuff). Stir for about 2 minutes and add a couple cranks of black pepper (¼ teaspoon).
    ½ cup freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano
    1 tablespoon butter
    ¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

### Mushroom Risotto

7. Heat 1 tablespoon butter and 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. 
    1 tablespoon butter
    1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
8. Add ¼ cup chopped shallots and cook for 2 minutes until translucent.
    ¼ cup shallots or yellow onion, chopped
9. Add 8 ounces of sliced mushrooms, 1 tablespoon minced garlic, 1 teaspoon dry thyme and/or sage, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and season with ½ teaspoon kosher salt and ¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper. Stir occasionally and cook (simmer) for about 8 minutes. 
    8 ounces baby portobello (aka baby bellas, cremini, etc), thinly sliced to ¼"
    1 tablespoon minced garlic
    1 tablespoon soy sauce
    1 teaspoon fresh or dried thyme and/or sage
    ½ teaspoon kosher salt
    ¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
10. Once the mushrooms have absorbed all the liquids, taste them and add more salt if necessary. Remove from the saucepan and set aside. 
11. Next, cook the risotto as outlined in the Basic Risotto recipe above.
12. Once the risotto is ready in its creamy deliciousness, put the cooked mushrooms back in the risotto and stir it all together.
13. Sprinkle with some roughly chopped fresh flat-leaf Italian parsley and pour yourself a glass of Sauvignon Blanc. You deserve it. 
    ½ cup roughly chopped flat leaf Italian parsley
